Biodiversity

The natural variety of flora and fauna in any given area, which sustain each other in an ecosystem.

The health of these ecosystems is key to the survival of all life on the planet. If one link is broken, the whole chain could collapse.

But, even as we speak, Earth's biodiversity is under threat from the planet's most intelligent species...

...Humans

warning

Human activity

The primary driver of biodiversity's global decline: a decline threatening the very planet on which we rely upon.

Without a shift in human behaviour, the global ecosystem will shatter.
And humanity will go with it.

Large mammal extinctions

A series of extinctions in megafauna mammal species, occuring approximately 12,000-52,000 years ago.

Drag the slider to see humanity's spread across the world.

This extinction irreparably damaged the Earth's diversity in mammals.

Holocene & future

Humans today remain the leading killer of biodiversity, and the situation appears to only be worsening.

We are currently in the midst of the Holocene Extinction

This gigantic event has brought extinction rates up to 1000x the previous natural background rate, and is rapidly increasing.

Human activity is the key player in the Holocene

Human expansion

The most potent destruction: as humans connect with the physical environment, whole ecosystems are threatened.

And humanity is expanding more rapidly than ever.

Land/sea use change
